vlemed

Description

The VLEMED community started as the Medical Diagnosis and Imaging subprogramme (SP1.3) of the Virtual Laboratory for e-Science (VL-e) project. The aim of the VL-e project was to bridge the gap between the technology push of the high performance networking and the Grid and the application pull of a wide range of scientific experimental applications.

Since 2008 the community is centred at the Academic Medical Centre of the University of Amsterdam (AMC) and lead by the e-Bioscience group of the Bioinformatics Laboratory. This group maintains the e-BioInfrastructure, a layer on top of the BiGGrid infrastructure, the Dutch National Grid Infrastructure. The target audience of the services are the Bioinformatics and Medical Imaging researchers at the AMC and close collaborators.

Services

  • How to get started
  • VBrowser development and support , see Documentation
  • Web based interface for executing data analysis on the grid
  • Support for porting applications to the Grid using workflow technology (MOTEUR)
  • Monitoring and support for execution of data analysis experiments on the Dutch grid
